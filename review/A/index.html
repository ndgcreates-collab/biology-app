Big Idea A: Distribution & Abundance of Organisms

The distribution and abundance of organisms is determined by interactions between organisms, and between organisms and the non-living environment.

Population Dynamics & Limiting Factors

⚠ Draft — verify on CPALMS

A population's size changes based on births, deaths, immigration, and emigration. Every environment has a carrying capacity — the maximum population size it can sustain — set by limiting factors such as food, water, space, disease, and predation.

Symbiosis & Species Interactions

⚠ Draft — verify on CPALMS

Organisms interact with each other in patterns that shape where and how abundantly they live: mutualism (both benefit), commensalism (one benefits, the other unaffected), parasitism (one benefits, one harmed), predation (one eats the other), and competition (both are limited by sharing a resource).
